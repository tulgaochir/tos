Free Cash Flow (FCF)

Free cash flow is the cash available after essential reinvestment. It's crucial for valuation.

Free Cash Flow to the Firm (FCFF)

The cash available to all investors (debt and equity holders).

FCFF = CFO + Interest √ó (1 ‚àí Tax Rate) ‚àí FCInv

Or from Net Income:

FCFF = NI + NCC + Interest √ó (1 ‚àí Tax Rate) ‚àí FCInv ‚àí WCInv

Where: NI = Net Income, NCC = Non-Cash Charges, FCInv = Fixed Capital Investment, WCInv = Working Capital Investment.

FCFF Example

CFO = $500K, Interest = $50K, Tax Rate = 30%, FCInv = $120K

After-tax interest = $50K √ó (1 ‚àí 0.30) = $35K

FCFF = $500K + $35K ‚àí $120K = $415K

Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE)

The cash available to common shareholders after all obligations.

FCFE = CFO ‚àí FCInv + Net Borrowing

Or from Net Income:

FCFE = NI + NCC ‚àí FCInv ‚àí WCInv + Net Borrowing

Net Borrowing = New Debt Issued ‚àí Debt Repaid

FCFE Example

CFO = $500K, FCInv = $120K, New Debt = $80K, Debt Repaid = $50K

Net Borrowing = $80K ‚àí $50K = $30K

FCFE = $500K ‚àí $120K + $30K = $410K

Key Cash Flow Ratios

Ratios help assess performance, risk, and sustainability.

Performance Ratios

Cash Flow to Revenue

CFO / Net Revenue

Purpose: Measures how much operating cash is generated per dollar of sales.

Example: CFO = $150K, Revenue = $1M ‚Üí Ratio = 15% ‚Üí $0.15 cash per $1 of revenue.

Cash Return on Assets (CROA)

CFO / Average Total Assets

Purpose: Measures cash efficiency of asset investment.

Example: CFO = $150K, Avg Assets = $2M ‚Üí 7.5%

Cash Return on Equity (CROE)

CFO / Average Shareholders' Equity

Purpose: Measures cash return to owners.

Example: CFO = $150K, Avg Equity = $800K ‚Üí 18.75%

Cash to Income

CFO / Operating Income

Purpose: Assesses quality of earnings.

Example: CFO = $150K, Operating Income = $140K ‚Üí Ratio = 1.07 ‚Üí strong conversion.

Cash Flow per Share

(CFO ‚àí Preferred Dividends) / Weighted Avg Shares

Purpose: Operating cash flow on a per-share basis.

Example: CFO = $150K, Pref Div = $10K, Shares = 100K ‚Üí $1.40/share

Coverage Ratios

Debt Coverage

CFO / Total Debt

Purpose: Ability to cover total debt with operating cash.

Example: CFO = $150K, Debt = $500K ‚Üí 30%

Interest Coverage

(CFO + Interest Paid + Taxes Paid) / Interest Paid

Purpose: Ability to meet interest obligations.

Example: CFO = $150K, Int = $30K, Taxes = $40K ‚Üí ($220K)/$30K = 7.33x

Reinvestment Ratio

CFO / Cash Paid for Long-Term Assets

Purpose: Can operations fund asset purchases?

Example: CFO = $150K, CapEx = $100K ‚Üí 1.5x

Debt Payment Coverage

CFO / Debt Repayments

Purpose: Ability to pay down debt.

Example: CFO = $150K, Debt Paid = $75K ‚Üí 2.0x

Dividend Payment Coverage

CFO / Dividends Paid

Purpose: Sustainability of dividend payouts.

Example: CFO = $150K, Dividends = $50K ‚Üí 3.0x

Investing & Financing Coverage

CFO / (Investing + Financing Outflows)

Purpose: Can operations fund all external outflows?

Example: CFO = $150K, Outflows = $180K ‚Üí 0.83 ‚Üí deficit
