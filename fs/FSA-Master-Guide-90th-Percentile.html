2.2 Revenue Recognition (5-Step Model)

IFRS 15 / ASC 606 - The 5 Steps:
  1. Identify the contract with a customer
  2. Identify the performance obligations
  3. Determine the transaction price
  4. Allocate the transaction price to performance obligations
  5. Recognize revenue when (or as) control transfers

2.3 Earnings Per Share (EPS) - CRITICAL FORMULAS

Basic EPS

Basic EPS = (Net Income ‚àí Preferred Dividends) / Weighted Average Common Shares Outstanding

Subtract preferred dividends - they belong to preferred shareholders

Diluted EPS - Stock Options (Treasury Stock Method)

Proceeds = Options √ó Exercise Price
Shares Repurchased = Proceeds / Average Market Price
Net New Shares = Options Exercised ‚àí Shares Repurchased

Only dilutive if Market Price > Exercise Price

Diluted EPS - Convertible Debt

Adjusted NI = NI + (Interest Expense √ó (1 ‚àí Tax Rate))
New Shares = (Debt Amount / Par) √ó Conversion Ratio
Diluted EPS = Adjusted NI / (Weighted Avg Shares + New Shares)

Add back after-tax interest saved from conversion

Diluted EPS - Convertible Preferred

Adjusted NI = NI + Preferred Dividends
New Shares = (Preferred Shares) √ó Conversion Ratio
Diluted EPS = Adjusted NI / (Weighted Avg Shares + New Shares)

No preferred dividends if converted

‚ö†Ô∏è KEY RULE: Diluted EPS ‚â§ Basic EPS. Only include dilutive securities (those that DECREASE EPS). Anti-dilutive securities are excluded.

3.2 LIFO vs FIFO During Inflation - CRITICAL!

Metric FIFO (Rising Prices) LIFO (Rising Prices)
COGS Lower (old, cheaper costs) Higher (new, expensive costs)
Ending Inventory Higher (recent costs on B/S) Lower (old costs on B/S)
Gross Profit Higher Lower
Net Income Higher Lower
Taxes Higher Lower (tax advantage)
Cash Flow Lower (more taxes) Higher (tax savings)
üéØ Exam Tip: During DEFLATION, all effects reverse! LIFO ‚Üí Higher NI, FIFO ‚Üí Lower NI

3.3 LIFO Reserve & Conversions - HIGH YIELD

LIFO Reserve

LIFO Reserve = FIFO Inventory ‚àí LIFO Inventory

Disclosed in footnotes for U.S. companies using LIFO

Convert LIFO to FIFO

Inventory (FIFO) = Inventory (LIFO) + LIFO Reserve
COGS (FIFO) = COGS (LIFO) ‚àí ŒîLI FO Reserve
Retained Earnings (FIFO) = RE (LIFO) + LIFO Reserve √ó (1 ‚àí Tax Rate)

Subtract increase in reserve from COGS (or add decrease)

‚ö†Ô∏è LIFO Liquidation: When LIFO inventory layers from prior years (old, cheap costs) are sold ‚Üí Abnormally low COGS ‚Üí Inflated profits. This is unsustainable and low-quality earnings!

3.5 Long-Term Assets: Depreciation Methods

Straight-Line Depreciation

Annual Depreciation = (Cost ‚àí Salvage Value) / Useful Life

Most common. Equal expense each year.

Declining Balance (Accelerated)

Annual Depreciation = Book Value at Beginning √ó (2 / Useful Life)
Note: Salvage value NOT subtracted initially

Higher expenses early, lower later. Cannot depreciate below salvage value.

Units of Production

Depreciation per Unit = (Cost ‚àí Salvage Value) / Total Units Expected
Annual Depreciation = Depreciation per Unit √ó Units Produced This Year

Varies with production volume

üéØ Component Depreciation: IFRS REQUIRES depreciating significant components separately. U.S. GAAP allows but doesn't require.

3.6 Asset Impairment - IFRS vs U.S. GAAP

Aspect IFRS (IAS 36) U.S. GAAP (ASC 360)

‚ö†Ô∏è KEY DIFFERENCE: IFRS uses discounted cash flows (recoverable amount). U.S. GAAP uses undiscounted cash flows for recoverability test, then fair value for loss measurement. This makes U.S. GAAP less likely to trigger impairment.

3.8 Revaluation Model (IFRS Only)

IFRS Allows Revaluation Model for:

  • Intangible assets (if active market exists)

Revaluation Process:

Part 4: Liabilities & Equity Analysis

4.1 Lease Accounting - IFRS 16 / ASC 842

Lessee Perspective - Both Standards Similar Now:
  • Right-of-Use (ROU) Asset on balance sheet
  • Lease Liability = PV of lease payments
  • Exception: Short-term leases (<12 months) can be expensed

Initial Lease Liability

Lease Liability = PV of Lease Payments
= Œ£ (Payment_t / (1 + r)^t)

r = incremental borrowing rate (or implicit rate if known)

Finance Lease - Income Statement Impact

Depreciation Expense = ROU Asset / Lease Term
Interest Expense = Lease Liability √ó Discount Rate
Total Expense = Depreciation + Interest (front-loaded)

Operating Lease - Income Statement Impact (U.S. GAAP)

Lease Expense = Total Lease Payments / Lease Term (straight-line)

IFRS doesn't distinguish - all are finance leases for lessee

4.2 Bond Accounting

4.5 Income Taxes - CRITICAL FORMULAS

Income Tax Expense

4.6 Deferred Tax Assets & Liabilities

DTA (Deferred Tax Asset):
Created when: Taxable Income > Accounting Income
‚Üí Pay more tax now, less later
= Future tax benefit

Examples: Warranty provisions, Bad debt expense (allowance method), Net operating losses (NOLs)

DTL (Deferred Tax Liability):
Created when: Accounting Income > Taxable Income
‚Üí Pay less tax now, more later
= Future tax obligation

Examples: Accelerated depreciation for tax, Installment sales, Gains on available-for-sale securities

DTA/DTL Calculation

DTA or DTL = Temporary Difference √ó Tax Rate

Temporary Difference = Carrying Amount ‚àí Tax Base
‚ö†Ô∏è Valuation Allowance: If it's more likely than not that a DTA won't be realized ‚Üí Create a valuation allowance (contra-asset). This reduces the DTA and increases tax expense.

5.5 Liquidity Ratios

Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities

Should be >1. Measures ability to pay short-term obligations.
