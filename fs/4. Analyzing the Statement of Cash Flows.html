Introduction to the Statement of Cash Flows

The Statement of Cash Flows (SCF) shows how cash moves in and out of a company over a period (e.g., a quarter or year). It explains the change in the cash balance reported on the balance sheet.

Unlike the income statement (which uses accrual accounting), the SCF focuses on actual cash transactions.

üìä Income Statement

Revenue is recognized when earned, even if cash hasn't been received

üí∞ Statement of Cash Flows

Cash is recorded only when it is received or paid

Accrual vs. Cash

A company sells $10,000 of goods on credit in Q1.

  • ‚Ä¢ Income Statement: Records $10,000 revenue in Q1
  • ‚Ä¢ Cash Flow Statement: $0 cash from operations in Q1
  • ‚Ä¢ When the customer pays in Q2, the SCF records the $10,000 as cash received

Cash Flow from Operating Activities (CFO)

Operating activities are the core revenue-generating activities of a business (e.g., selling goods, paying suppliers).

There are two methods to present CFO:

The Indirect Method (Most Common)

Starts with net income and adjusts for non-cash items and changes in working capital.

1

Start with Net Income

2

Add back non-cash expenses

  • ‚Ä¢ Depreciation & Amortization
  • ‚Ä¢ Losses on asset sales
3

Subtract gains on asset sales

4

Adjust for changes in working capital

  • ‚Ä¢ ‚Üë Accounts Receivable ‚Üí Subtract (cash not received)
  • ‚Ä¢ ‚Üë Inventory ‚Üí Subtract (cash tied up)
  • ‚Ä¢ ‚Üë Accounts Payable ‚Üí Add (cash not yet paid)

The Direct Method

Shows actual cash inflows and outflows from operations.

Cash received from customers

Cash paid to suppliers

Cash paid to employees

Cash paid for interest and taxes

Note: U.S. GAAP and IFRS require the indirect method to be disclosed even if the direct method is used.

Calculating Direct Method Items

To convert accrual data to cash, use related balance sheet accounts.

1. Cash Received from Customers

Cash Received = Revenue ‚àí ŒîAccounts Receivable

Example

Revenue = $500,000, A/R ‚Üë by $20,000.

Cash Received = $500,000 ‚àí $20,000 = $480,000

2. Cash Paid for Inventory

Step 1: Calculate Purchases

Purchases = COGS + ŒîInventory

Step 2: Calculate Cash Paid

Cash Paid = Purchases ‚àí ŒîAccounts Payable

Example

COGS = $300,000, Inventory ‚Üë $10,000 ‚Üí Purchases = $310,000

A/P ‚Üì $5,000 ‚Üí Cash Paid = $310,000 ‚àí (‚àí$5,000) = $315,000

3. Cash Paid to Employees

Cash Paid = Salaries Expense ‚àí ŒîSalaries Payable

4. Cash Paid for Interest

Cash Paid = Interest Expense ‚àí ŒîInterest Payable

Converting Indirect to Direct Method

  1. 1. Start with accrual income statement items (e.g., revenue, COGS)
  2. 2. Remove non-cash components (e.g., depreciation)
  3. 3. Adjust for changes in related balance sheet accounts to get cash flows

Cash Flow from Financing Activities (CFF)

Financing activities involve transactions with owners and creditors.

‚úÖ Cash Inflows

  • ‚Ä¢ Issuing debt (bonds, loans)

‚ùå Cash Outflows

  • ‚Ä¢ Repaying debt (principal)
  • ‚Ä¢ Paying dividends
  • ‚Ä¢ Buying back stock (treasury stock)
Net CFF = Proceeds from Debt + Proceeds from Stock ‚àí Debt Repayments ‚àí Dividends ‚àí Stock Buybacks

Example

A company:

  • ‚Ä¢ Borrows $100,000
  • ‚Ä¢ Repays $30,000 of old debt

  • ‚Ä¢ Issues $50,000 in new stock

Critical Formulas - Master These

üìê Essential Cash Flow Formulas

1. Cash Received from Customers (Direct Method)

Cash Received = Revenue - Increase in A/R (or + Decrease in A/R)

If A/R increases, less cash was collected than revenue recognized

2. Cash Paid for Inventory (Direct Method)

Step 1: Purchases = COGS + Increase in Inventory
Step 2: Cash Paid = Purchases - Increase in A/P

If inventory increases, more was purchased than sold. If A/P increases, less cash was paid

3. CFO (Indirect Method) - Core Formula

CFO = Net Income
+ Depreciation & Amortization
+ Losses (- Gains) on Asset Sales
- Increase in A/R (+ Decrease in A/R)
- Increase in Inventory (+ Decrease in Inventory)
+ Increase in A/P (- Decrease in A/P)

Remember: Asset increases are uses of cash (subtract); Liability increases are sources of cash (add)

4. Free Cash Flow to the Firm (FCFF)

FCFF = CFO + Interest(1 - Tax Rate) - FCInv

Or: FCFF = NI + NCC + Interest(1-t) - FCInv - WCInv
FCInv = Fixed Capital Investment (net PP&E purchases)
WCInv = Working Capital Investment

5. Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE)

FCFE = CFO - FCInv + Net Borrowing

Cash available to equity holders after covering capex and debt obligations

6. Net Change in Cash

Œî Cash = CFO + CFI + CFF

Beginning Cash + Œî Cash = Ending Cash (must tie to balance sheet)

Common Exam Traps & Mistakes

‚ö†Ô∏è Trap #1: Working Capital Adjustment Direction

Mistake: Adding increase in A/R to net income when calculating CFO

Reality:

  • ‚Üë Current Assets (A/R, Inventory): SUBTRACT from NI (cash not received, cash tied up)
  • ‚Üë Current Liabilities (A/P): ADD to NI (cash not yet paid)
  • Rule: Asset increase = Use of cash (subtract); Liability increase = Source of cash (add)

‚ö†Ô∏è Trap #2: Depreciation Treatment

Mistake: Thinking depreciation is a cash outflow

Reality: Depreciation is NON-CASH

  • Added back to NI in indirect method because it reduced NI but involved no cash
  • Cash was spent when asset was PURCHASED (shows in CFI)
  • Depreciation just allocates that past cost over time

‚ö†Ô∏è Trap #3: Gains/Losses on Asset Sales

Mistake: Adding gains to CFO or subtracting losses from CFO

Reality: Do the OPPOSITE

  • Gain on sale: SUBTRACT from NI (investing activity, not operating)
  • Loss on sale: ADD to NI (remove non-operating loss)
  • Full sale proceeds: Go in CFI, not CFO

‚ö†Ô∏è Trap #4: Interest and Dividends Classification

Mistake: Assuming interest paid is always CFO

Reality: It depends on the standard

  • U.S. GAAP: Interest paid = CFO; Dividends paid = CFF
  • IFRS: Company can choose: Interest & Dividends paid can be CFO or CFF
  • Both standards: Dividends received = CFO; Interest received = CFO

‚ö†Ô∏è Trap #5: Direct vs. Indirect - Same CFO

Mistake: Thinking direct and indirect methods give different CFO totals

Reality: Both methods produce the SAME CFO amount

  • Only difference is PRESENTATION (format)
  • Indirect: starts with NI, adjusts
  • Direct: shows actual cash in/out

Memory Aids & Mnemonics

üß† Cash Flow Sections: "OIF"

"Only Investments Finance"

üß† Working Capital Rule: "ALSA"

Practice Questions

Question 1: CFO Indirect Method

A company reports Net Income of $100,000, Depreciation of $20,000, a $15,000 increase in A/R, and a $10,000 increase in A/P. What is CFO using the indirect method?

A) $115,000
B) $105,000
C) $125,000
Show Answer & Explanation

Answer: A) $115,000

Calculation:

  1. Start with NI: $100,000
  2. Add Depreciation (non-cash): +$20,000
  3. Subtract ‚Üë A/R (cash not collected): -$15,000
  4. Add ‚Üë A/P (cash not paid): +$10,000
  5. CFO = $100K + $20K - $15K + $10K = $115,000

Question 2: Cash Received from Customers

Revenue is $500,000 and A/R increased by $30,000 during the year. What is cash received from customers?

A) $530,000
B) $470,000
C) $500,000
Show Answer & Explanation

Answer: B) $470,000

Direct Method Formula:

Cash Received = Revenue - Œî A/R

Cash Received = $500,000 - $30,000 = $470,000

Why? A/R increased by $30K, meaning $30K of revenue was recognized but cash not yet collected

Question 3: Gain on Asset Sale

A company has NI of $200,000 and a $50,000 gain on sale of equipment. The equipment was sold for $150,000 cash. How should this be treated in the cash flow statement?

A) Add $50,000 to CFO
B) Subtract $50,000 from CFO; add $150,000 to CFI
C) Add $150,000 to CFO
Show Answer & Explanation

Answer: B) Subtract $50,000 from CFO; add $150,000 to CFI

Explanation:

  • CFO: Subtract $50K gain (it's included in NI but is not an operating activity)
  • CFI: Add full $150K proceeds from sale (investing activity)
  • The $50K gain = Sale proceeds ($150K) - Book value ($100K)

Key: Gains are removed from CFO; full proceeds go in CFI

Question 4: FCFE Calculation

CFO = $300,000; Fixed capital investment = $100,000; Net borrowing = $50,000. What is FCFE?

A) $250,000
B) $150,000
C) $450,000
Show Answer & Explanation

Answer: A) $250,000

Formula:

FCFE = CFO - FCInv + Net Borrowing

FCFE = $300,000 - $100,000 + $50,000 = $250,000

Interpretation: $250K is available to equity holders after covering capital investments and accounting for debt changes

Question 5: Cash Paid for Inventory

COGS = $400,000; Inventory increased by $20,000; A/P increased by $15,000. What was cash paid for inventory?

A) $405,000
B) $435,000
C) $385,000
Show Answer & Explanation

Answer: A) $405,000

Two-Step Calculation:

  1. Purchases = COGS + Œî Inventory = $400K + $20K = $420K
  2. Cash Paid = Purchases - Œî A/P = $420K - $15K = $405K

Why? Bought $420K but only paid $405K because A/P increased (deferred $15K payment)
